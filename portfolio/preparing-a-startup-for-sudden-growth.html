Preparing a startup for sudden growth

When a biomentric authentication startup has spent 6 years in R&D and is finally ready to hit the market, a lot of things need to be in place.

Contract, May 2017 - July 2018 (14 months)

The setup

After years of secret research and development, iProov was hitting the market. Unable to manually handle the influx of new customers, I was hired as an all-round UX, UI and engineer to come in and create an integrated and streamlined customer onboarding process.

Defining the problem

We worked together to establish a clearly articulated problem statement:

How can we enable the company to support the sudden rapid growth in customer base?

Flip it on its head, break it down:

  • Who are the customers?
  • What are the current bottlenecks and pain points?
  • How can we use technology to enable efffective self-service?

My approach

First, we had to understand the customers and different types of client relationships.

After many sessions with the leadership and sales team, I was able to uncover and document some clear answers.

Key tasks

  • Workshops with business, sales and technical teams
  • Interviews with internal stakeholders and resellers

Outcomes

  • A clear and shared understanding of different possible client relationship structures
  • A clear and shared understanding of how reporting and billing would work through a recursive reseller model

Second, we had to understand the internal bottlenecks and pain points.

Working with the sales and technical managers, I mapped out the entire onboarding process â€” from the legal paperwork, to deploying proof-of-concept service integrations, to billing and invoicing customers.

Outcomes

  • A thorough mapping of the the original manual workflows, with their weak points identified
  • A clearly documented proposal for solving the bottlenecks and pain points

Then we had to understand how we could architect a scalable business support system

The system needed to encompass the following:

  • Pre-sales
  • Sales
  • Onboarding
  • Technical support
  • SLA reporting
  • Billing and invoicing

Key tasks

  • Workshops with business, sales and technical teams
  • Reviewing API and developer documentation
  • Calls with sales and support teams to get answers
  • Creating rapid prototypes for service integrations as POCs

Outcomes
